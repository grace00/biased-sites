CS 106A

Welcome to Programming Methodologies! This class introduces computer programming for people who have not programmed before.

Course Info

Topics

Students will learn all the important topics of basic programming in Python: types, numbers, strings, functions, dictionaries, logic, decomposition, good programming style, debugging, and more. They’ll also touch on more advanced topics they might use in the future, including lambdas, comprehensions, modules, and Jupyter notebooks.

Python is a huge language with many advanced features, and CS106A does not cover all of it. CS106A teaches the important core features, and students will be able to solve real programming problems with just this course.

Section

There will be a weekly section, and signups begin the first week. Students will have their own section leader for the quarter who will lead section and grade their homework assignments.

Resources

We'll provide web pages of Python language reference, and there is no required textbook.

Grading

Homework is 35%, section is 5%, and exams are 60%

Assignments

Students will have 5 programming assignments.

Name Description Due
Karel the Robot Help Karel retrieve its newspaper 4/13
Breakout Recreate a fun game 5/2
Hangman Make a classic word game 5/16
Sand Implement a 2D world of sand 5/30
NameSurfer Graph the popularity of baby names 6/13

Lateness

Work turned in later after the deadline loses 15% per day. For example, if a student turns in their work one day late, and that work would have received a 90% had they turned it in on time, they will get 75% after the penalty.
